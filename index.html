BIOGRAPHY

I didn't start out life wanting to be a developer. I always wanted to be this until I wanted to be that, and so forth. I took up TaeKwonDo for a couple years. I switched to piano for the next 4. For the longest time, I wanted my mother to send me to China so I could learn Buddism as a Shaolin Monk(I say Buddism but really I wanted Kung Fu and would willingly be devout Buddist to get that). I wanted to be an Accupuncturist since the beginning of Middle School. I had a change of heart in High School and decided Engineering (I also taught myself guitar, ukulele, some accordian, and anything I could get my hands on). Tried Engineering in college(while learning JiuJitsu and Aikido, tried Art in college, took one C++ programming class as I tried Engineering again . . .

Anyways, the point isn't about how scattered my heart is. I like to think of it as a journey of self discovery. I may never know what truly satisfies my heart (besides my wife, I mean). I may never find the one thing society tells us we need to find, to define our humanity, our lives, our purpose. . .but I think that's rubbish. We're all from different walks of life. Within me, I feel like there's all sorts of things I've yet to discover. Different walks of my life.

Each part of me loves to create...Music, Art, Philosophy, Science, Politics, and more. Programming can be a tool to gain access to, and further each of my passions.

Oh and I still can't wait to visit China someday.
